what_i_learned: |
  Today began with a meeting covering timesheets, administrative paperwork,
  and other program requirements. We also participated in SAIRI Connect, where
  we had the opportunity to interact with students from other research groups
  through a series of games and activities.

  Later, our mentors organized a picnic-style discussion with Group 9, giving us
  a chance to learn more about each other's projects and prepare for the upcoming
  Mid-Summer Symposium. We discussed the machine learning models being used across
  different projects and compared how model performance was evaluated.

  One interesting takeaway was learning about the use of R² score in Group 9's
  project. Unlike our classification-focused QSAR work, their project involved
  predicting trends and continuous values, making regression metrics more
  appropriate than metrics such as F1 score. We also discussed why certain
  algorithms, including decision trees, were not well suited for their specific
  problem and dataset.

  Toward the end of the day, Ramisa reviewed upcoming tasks for our group and
  assigned new work. I was tasked with data cleaning and began working with a
  blood-brain barrier (BBB) dataset that will be used in future stages of our
  research.

blockers: |
  No major blockers today. Most of the challenges involved understanding how
  different machine learning problems require different evaluation metrics and
  modeling approaches.

reflection: |
  Today was valuable because it exposed me to research beyond my own project.
  Comparing our work with other groups helped me better understand the distinction
  between classification and regression problems and why model evaluation must be
  tailored to the specific research question. I'm looking forward to working with
  the BBB dataset and continuing to refine our data preparation pipeline.
