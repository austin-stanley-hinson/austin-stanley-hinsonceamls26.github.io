what_i_learned: |
  Today was our field trip to Washington, DC for the AWS Summit. It was also my
  first time visiting the capital, which made the experience even more exciting.

  I attended several sessions and learned a lot about cloud computing, artificial
  intelligence, and large language models. The talks helped me better understand
  how companies are using AWS tools and AI systems to build real-world products
  and improve their workflows.

  I also had the opportunity to connect with companies, professionals, and
  executives at the summit. I asked about their experiences, career paths, and how
  they use technology in their work. Some conversations were very helpful and gave
  me a better sense of the different directions I could take within computer
  science.

blockers: |
  Networking was the biggest challenge today. Some people were not very responsive,
  and a few conversations ended quickly after I introduced myself. It was a little
  uncomfortable, but it helped me understand that networking takes practice and
  confidence.

reflection: |
  Today pushed me to think more seriously about my own path in computer science.
  Some of the questions I was asked made me realize that I need to reflect more on
  what I want to do with my CS education instead of simply following what everyone
  else seems to be doing. Overall, the AWS Summit was a valuable experience because
  it exposed me to industry work, helped me practice networking, and gave me more
  motivation to define my own direction.
