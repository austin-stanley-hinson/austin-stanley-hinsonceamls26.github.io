what_i_learned: |
  Today began with a project overview session led by my faculty mentor, Dr. Roshan
  Paudel. He provided a breakdown of the tools and technologies that will be used
  throughout our Alzheimer's drug discovery project, including DiffDock, PubChem,
  AlphaFold 3, and other computational biology resources. The session helped me
  better understand the workflow we will follow to identify and evaluate potential
  drug candidates.

  We also worked through an AI Quest activity from Google that focused on applying
  artificial intelligence to real-world scenarios. Through the exercises, I learned
  more about selecting appropriate AI approaches for different problems and the
  considerations involved when choosing and evaluating models.

  Later, I attended Python 102 with Michael and continued my Gradio studies. We
  discussed APIs and their role in modern software development, particularly how
  applications communicate with external services. Building on that knowledge, we
  integrated the Groq API into a Gradio application and created a simple chat app
  using gr.Blocks. It was exciting to see how a few lines of code could connect a
  user interface to a powerful language model.

blockers: |
  No major blockers today. There were several new technologies and tools introduced,
  but the sessions provided enough context to understand how they fit into the
  broader research and development workflow.

reflection: |
  Today helped connect many of the concepts I've been learning over the past week.
  Understanding the technologies behind our research project gave me a clearer
  picture of the work ahead, while the API and Gradio exercises showed how AI
  models can be integrated into practical applications. I'm looking forward to
  gaining more hands-on experience with these tools as the project progresses.
