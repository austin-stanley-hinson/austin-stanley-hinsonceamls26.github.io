what_i_learned: |
  Today was a full day focused on research and coding. We spent time working
  through QSAR modeling concepts, discussing different machine learning models,
  when they should be used, and what role they play in the drug discovery
  process.

  Ramisa guided us through a coding assignment that initially felt challenging,
  but after she revisited the workflow and explained the concepts step by step,
  everything became much clearer. Her demonstration used data retrieved from
  PubChem, which helped reinforce the overall QSAR pipeline.

  As part of the assignment, I explored alternative molecular databases and
  chose ChEMBL. Working with ChEMBL was quite different from PubChem because
  the API returned nested dictionaries rather than the simpler JSON structure
  used in the PubChem example. Additionally, compound properties and labels were
  organized differently, requiring extra effort to identify the relevant fields
  and clean the data properly.

  Despite these differences, I successfully retrieved, cleaned, and ranked
  compounds using a simple scoring approach. This experience gave me a better
  understanding of the challenges involved in working with real-world biological
  datasets and adapting code to different data sources.
