what_i_learned: |
  Today included SAIRI Connect, where we helped plan for the upcoming field day.
  After that, I focused on the molecular docking and structure-based validation
  part of our Alzheimer's drug discovery project.

  I worked on preparing the AChE protein receptor for docking because AChE is the
  primary target for our final candidate set. I downloaded the AChE crystal
  structure file, opened it in ChimeraX, and separated the co-crystallized
  donepezil-like reference ligand from the protein. I then cleaned the receptor by
  removing the extra chain, water molecules, ions, sugars, and the reference
  ligand, leaving a cleaned AChE receptor structure ready for docking.

  After cleaning the receptor, I moved the workflow into Google Colab to make the
  docking setup easier. I used the saved donepezil reference ligand to calculate
  the docking grid center, which gave coordinates around center_x = -1.582,
  center_y = -50.192, and center_z = 2.101. I used a docking box size of
  22 x 22 x 22 Å.

  I then prepared the cleaned AChE receptor into a Vina-compatible .pdbqt file
  using Meeko. There were several setup issues along the way, including missing
  dependencies like RDKit, gemmi, and ProDy, as well as alternate atom locations
  in the receptor structure. I fixed the alternate-location issue by keeping the
  standard A conformations and removing duplicate alternate positions, which
  allowed Meeko to successfully generate the prepared receptor file.

  I also recreated the ligand preparation workflow for the 12 prioritized docking
  candidates. Starting from the final top-12 docking candidate CSV, I used RDKit
  to generate 3D ligand structures and Open Babel to convert them into .pdbqt
  files for docking. All 12 candidate ligands were successfully converted into
  Vina-ready ligand files.

  By the end of the session, I had the prepared AChE receptor, the 12 prepared
  ligand files, and the docking grid configuration ready. I then started running
  AutoDock Vina in Colab to dock all 12 compounds against the AChE binding site.
  This moved the project from QSAR-based candidate prioritization into actual
  structure-based validation.

blockers: |
  The main blockers today were technical setup issues in the docking workflow.
  Some required dependencies, including RDKit, gemmi, and ProDy, were missing at
  first. I also had to resolve alternate atom locations in the receptor structure
  before Meeko could successfully prepare the receptor for docking.

reflection: |
  Today felt like an important transition point in the project. Earlier work
  focused on cleaning datasets, training QSAR models, and ranking promising
  compounds. Today, I moved into structure-based validation by preparing the AChE
  receptor, generating ligand files, defining the docking grid, and beginning the
  AutoDock Vina docking process.

  I learned that molecular docking requires careful preparation before any docking
  scores can be trusted. Cleaning the receptor, preserving the binding site,
  preparing ligands correctly, and choosing a reasonable grid center are all
  important steps that affect the quality of the final docking results.
