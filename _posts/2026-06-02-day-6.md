what_i_learned: |
Today began with a research group meeting where we discussed our progress and
upcoming project milestones. Following that, we participated in a writing session
focused on literature reviews. I learned more about the purpose of a literature
review, how to synthesize findings from multiple sources, and the importance of
identifying gaps in existing research.

We then attended an Amazon career exploration session that introduced several
concepts behind large-scale internet infrastructure. Topics included content
delivery networks (CDNs), data centers, and how companies stream content efficiently
across long distances to millions of users. The session was interactive and included
Kahoot quizzes that helped reinforce many of the concepts discussed.

Later, I continued Python 102 with Michael. Building on our previous work with
APIs and Gradio, we created a game powered by the Groq API and developed additional
user interfaces using Gradio. The project provided more hands-on experience with
connecting AI models to interactive applications and reinforced concepts related
to APIs and frontend design.

blockers: |
No major blockers today. Some of the cloud infrastructure concepts were new to me,
but the examples and quizzes helped make them easier to understand.

reflection: |
Today exposed me to several different aspects of research and technology. The
literature review session helped strengthen my understanding of academic research,
while the Amazon session provided insight into the systems that power large-scale
internet services. Ending the day by building AI-powered applications with Gradio
was a fun way to apply programming concepts and continue developing practical
software engineering skills.
