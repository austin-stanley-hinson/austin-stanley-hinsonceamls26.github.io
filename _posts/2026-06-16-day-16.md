what_i_learned: |
  Today began with a research group meeting where I presented a paper I had read
  on natural compounds and herbal extracts that have been tested as potential
  candidates for slowing cognitive decline in Alzheimer's disease. Some of the
  compounds discussed included Ginkgo biloba, saffron, and other natural products
  that have been studied in clinical trials.

  One major takeaway from the paper was that while some compounds showed promising
  results, many trials were unsuccessful or difficult to compare. The studies were
  highly heterogeneous, which made it challenging to perform a complete meta-analysis
  across all 35 trials.

  I also listened to literature review presentations from my teammates, including
  Jonathan, Ranjish, and Destiny, and learned more about the different directions
  their papers explored.

  After lunch, we moved into another dataset creation session. Each of us was
  assigned a target to investigate, and I worked on APOE. I searched for APOE-related
  datasets on PubChem and began cleaning, organizing, and analyzing the data for
  future QSAR modeling. This work involved identifying useful compound information,
  preparing the dataset, and thinking through how it could fit into our project
  workflow.

blockers: |
  The main challenge today was working through the dataset creation process for
  APOE. Finding useful PubChem data, cleaning it, and making it suitable for
  modeling required careful attention and will take more work to complete.

reflection: |
  Today helped connect the literature review side of the project with the dataset
  and modeling side. Presenting on natural compounds gave me more background on
