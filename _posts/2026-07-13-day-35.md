what_i_learned: |
  Today was focused on drafting the literature review section for our main paper
  and continuing to read more research papers related to our project.

  One of the papers I read helped me think more clearly about possible extensions
  for our work. I discussed these ideas with Ramisa, especially how we could move
  beyond QSAR ranking and docking into stronger validation steps.

  One possible extension is using GROMACS, a free and open-source molecular
  dynamics software suite, to run 20ns or longer molecular dynamics simulations
  on our final top-ranked compounds across all targets. This could help measure
  binding stability and provide more insight into whether the compound-protein
  interactions remain stable over time.

  Another possible extension is using AlzyFinder, a web-based Alzheimer's disease
  screening platform driven by XGBoost machine learning models. This could help
  support multi-target screening and provide another way to compare or validate
  our top-ranked compounds.

  We also began planning our commercial video for the final symposium. The goal of
  the video is to introduce the project clearly and make the work understandable
  to a broader audience.

blockers: |
  There were no major blockers today, but it was challenging to decide which
  extensions were realistic for our current project timeline and which ones would
  be better framed as future work.

reflection: |
  Today helped me see how the literature review can do more than summarize past
  research. It can also help identify gaps, justify our workflow, and suggest
  meaningful next steps for the project. Discussing possible extensions with
  Ramisa helped me better understand how molecular dynamics, binding stability
  analysis, and multi-target screening could strengthen the project beyond the
  current QSAR and docking workflow.
