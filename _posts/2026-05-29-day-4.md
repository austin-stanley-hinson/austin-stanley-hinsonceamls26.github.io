what_i_learned: |
  Today began with a research session with my group where I presented my assigned
  research paper. Through the presentation, I gained a deeper understanding of the
  biochemistry behind Alzheimer's disease, including the roles of amyloid-beta (Aβ),
  amyloid precursor protein (APP), tau, phosphorylated tau (p-tau), and the
  blood-brain barrier (BBB). I also explored why alternative delivery methods,
  such as epidural administration, are not more widely investigated as a means of
  bypassing the BBB for Alzheimer's therapeutics.

  Later, we had a session on Generative AI and Large Language Models where we
  discussed concepts such as tokenization, embeddings, and how language models
  process and represent information. The interactive Kahoot activities made the
  session particularly engaging, and I enjoyed the challenge of distinguishing
  AI-generated images from real ones.

  I also continued the Gradio lessons with Michael and learned how to create
  interfaces for Python applications. It was exciting to see how quickly simple,
  functional web applications can be built using Python.
