what_i_learned: |
Today began with a research session with my group where I presented my assigned
research paper. Through the presentation, I gained a deeper understanding of the
biochemistry behind Alzheimer's disease, including the roles of amyloid-beta (Aβ),
amyloid precursor protein (APP), tau, phosphorylated tau (p-tau), and the
blood-brain barrier (BBB). One question that particularly interested me was why
epidural delivery methods are not more extensively explored as a way to bypass
the BBB for Alzheimer's therapeutics.

Later, we had a session on Generative AI and Large Language Models. We discussed
concepts such as tokenization, embeddings, and how language models represent and
process information. The interactive Kahoot activities made the learning process
engaging, and I especially enjoyed the challenge of distinguishing AI-generated
images from real ones.

I also continued the Gradio lessons with Michael and learned how to create simple
interfaces for Python applications. It was exciting to see how quickly functional
web interfaces can be built with relatively little code.

blockers: |
No major blockers today. Some of the research papers were initially intimidating
and difficult to understand, but taking the time to look up unfamiliar concepts
and terminology helped me work through them and gain a better understanding of
the material.

reflection: |
Today was one of the most intellectually engaging days of the program so far.
Presenting a research paper pushed me to understand complex biological concepts
more deeply, while the Generative AI sessions exposed me to ideas that are
increasingly important in modern computing and research. I left the day feeling
challenged but also more confident in my ability to learn unfamiliar material
and connect ideas across disciplines.
