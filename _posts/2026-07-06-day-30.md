what_i_learned: |
  Today I focused on redoing my QSAR modeling workflow by engineering a teaching
  rule to rank compounds more clearly. The goal was to create a more meaningful
  way to label and prioritize compounds based on molecular properties, instead of
  only relying on the raw dataset labels.

  I also worked on improving the modeling process by dealing more carefully with
  class imbalance. This was important because imbalanced datasets can make a model
  look accurate while still performing poorly on the smaller class. To address
  this, I tested models that were new to me, including Balanced Random Forest,
  while comparing performance across different approaches.

  By the end of the day, I was able to rebuild the workflow, generate ranked
  compounds, and begin drafting the relevant QSAR modeling section for our main
  paper.

blockers: |
  The main blocker today was having to redo the entire workflow while also trying
  to balance model accuracy with methods I was less familiar with, such as
  Balanced Random Forest. It took time to understand how these models handled
  imbalance and how to compare them fairly.

reflection: |
  Today was challenging but productive. Redoing the workflow helped me better
  understand how much preprocessing, labeling, and class balance can affect QSAR
  results. It also gave me stronger material to contribute to the modeling section
  of our paper because I now have a clearer explanation of how the compounds were
  ranked and how imbalance was handled during training.
