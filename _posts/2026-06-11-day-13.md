what_i_learned: |
  Today was spent entirely in the research lab as we continued searching for
  suitable datasets for our Alzheimer's drug discovery project. Ramisa tasked
  each team member with identifying potential datasets that could be used for
  QSAR modeling and virtual screening.

  During the search process, Ranjish and I initially identified the same dataset,
  so I was asked to find an additional source. I explored several datasets on
  Kaggle and evaluated them based on the molecular properties and descriptors
  required for our project. Some datasets contained the columns we needed but
  were significantly larger than necessary, requiring additional filtering and
  preprocessing. Others were missing important features, which led me to track
  down their original sources and retrieve the relevant data directly.

  Throughout the day, I gained a better appreciation for the challenges involved
  in dataset selection, validation, and preparation. We also began preparing
  slides for our upcoming weekly showcase presentation.

blockers: |
  The biggest challenge today came when we learned that PubChem would need to be
  our primary data source moving forward. Much of the work completed on datasets
  derived from other sources had to be reconsidered, requiring us to restart our
  search and identify suitable datasets that could be traced directly back to
  PubChem. This meant revisiting several decisions and adapting our workflow to
  align with the project's requirements.

reflection: |
  Although it was frustrating to revisit work that had already been completed,
  today highlighted the importance of data provenance and using appropriate
  sources in research. The experience reinforced that finding the right dataset
  is often just as important as building the model itself. While progress felt
  slower than expected, I left the day with a better understanding of the data
  requirements for our project and a clearer direction moving forward.
