what_i_learned: |
  Today was mostly focused on reading more papers on molecular docking, writing
  literature reviews, and presenting our findings with my colleagues.

  One paper I reviewed analyzed curcumin derivatives and their potential role in
  breaking down amyloid-beta plaques in the brain. The paper used docking to study
  compound interactions and also discussed limitations, including the restricted
  number of poses docking can explore when ligands interact with protein targets.

  A second paper identified three promising compounds that advanced beyond docking
  into molecular dynamics analysis. The study also used kinase cross-docking and
  consensus docking, which helped me understand how researchers can compare and
  validate compound binding across different docking approaches.

blockers: |

reflection: |
  Today helped me better understand why docking is useful after QSAR ranking. It
  provides another layer of analysis by testing whether top compounds may bind
  well to target proteins. I also learned that docking results have limitations,
  so methods like molecular dynamics, cross-docking, and consensus docking can
  help strengthen confidence in promising compounds.
