what_i_learned: |
  Today began with a research group meeting where Ramisa helped us review and
  refine our weekly showcase slides. We focused on improving the accuracy of our
  content and making sure our progress throughout the week was communicated
  clearly.

  We then attended a workshop on Large Language Models (LLMs), where we learned
  more about how these models work, their capabilities, and some of the
  considerations involved when using them effectively. The session provided a
  useful overview of the technology that powers many modern AI applications.

  Later, Dr. Mack introduced us to Overleaf and demonstrated how it can be used
  for collaborative scientific writing and research documentation. This was my
  first formal introduction to the platform, and I can already see how useful it
  will be for future research papers and reports.

  The day concluded with our weekly showcase presentations. Watching the other
  groups present gave me a much better understanding of the variety of projects
  being conducted across the program and the different approaches students are
  taking to solve their research problems.

blockers: |
  Our group struggled with time management during the showcase presentation and
  ran out of time before we could fully discuss our next steps and future plans.

reflection: |
  Today's showcase highlighted the importance of communicating research clearly
  and efficiently. While our presentation could have been paced better, I learned
  a great deal from seeing the work of other groups and reflecting on our own
  progress. The LLM and Overleaf sessions also introduced tools that will be
  valuable throughout the remainder of the program and beyond.
