what_i_learned: |
  Today started with me providing my ranked compounds and model comparison results
  for my QSAR workflow. The workflow had three main modeling tasks: classification,
  regression, and promising label prediction.

  For the classification task, Random Forest performed best. For the regression
  task, Random Forest Regressor performed best. For the promising label task,
  Balanced Random Forest performed best because it handled the class imbalance
  better than the other models.

  I also read two more papers on molecular docking and continued building my
  understanding of how docking is used after QSAR modeling to evaluate compound
  binding with Alzheimer's-related targets.

  Later in the day, I sat through literature review presentations from my
  teammates. Jonathan's paper was especially interesting because it discussed a
  compound from bilberry that was investigated for Alzheimer's disease and
  performed very well. It was cool to see another natural compound appear in the
  docking and Alzheimer's literature, especially after reading earlier papers on
  curcumin derivatives.

blockers: |
  There were no major technical blockers today, but organizing the different model
  comparison results across classification, regression, and promising label tasks
  required careful attention.

reflection: |
  Today helped me connect my modeling workflow more clearly to the next stage of
  the project. Providing ranked compounds and comparing models made the QSAR work
  feel more complete, while the docking papers showed how those ranked compounds
  can be further investigated for potential binding performance.

  The movie session with the group was also a fun break. It was nice spending time
  with people from the other groups outside the usual research setting.
